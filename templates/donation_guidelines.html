Donation Guidelines

1. Donate Fresh and Safe Food +
Only donate food that is fresh, properly stored, and within its expiry date. Avoid perishable items that require refrigeration unless specified.
2. Proper Packaging +
Use clean, sealed containers or eco-friendly packaging to prevent contamination and ensure safe transportation.
3. Labeling is Important +
Label the donation with important details like type of food, date of preparation, and any allergens (nuts, dairy, etc.).
4. Coordinate Before Donating +
Please contact our team in advance to ensure proper collection and avoid food wastage due to scheduling issues.
5. Respect Hygiene Protocols +
All food should be prepared and handled in a hygienic environment. Use gloves, hairnets, and sanitized utensils during preparation.
6. No Expired or Spoiled Items +
Do not donate any food that is spoiled, expired, or questionable in terms of safety and quality.
7. Separate Veg and Non-Veg +
Clearly separate vegetarian and non-vegetarian items and label them appropriately to respect dietary restrictions.
8. Inform About Quantity +
Mention the approximate quantity of food you are donating to help us plan distribution better.
9. Timely Delivery +
Please ensure the food is donated within the appropriate time window to avoid spoilage and ensure freshness.
10. Follow Local Guidelines +
Follow any local regulations and community guidelines related to food safety and donation practices.
