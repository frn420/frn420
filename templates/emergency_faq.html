Emergency FAQ

1. What qualifies as an emergency donation? +
An emergency donation refers to any food or supplies urgently needed to assist people in crisis situations such as disasters or urgent community needs.
2. How fast do I need to donate? +
We request donations within 2-6 hours of identifying the emergency. Quick donations can save lives!
3. Can I donate food that needs refrigeration? +
Yes, but please ensure it is properly packaged with ice packs or cooled storage to maintain freshness during transportation.
4. How do I ensure my donation is safely transported? +
Coordinate with our emergency response team, and we will arrange for the food to be collected and transported to the right place.
5. Can I donate both cooked and uncooked food? +
Yes, both cooked and uncooked food can be donated, but please ensure they are packaged securely to prevent contamination.
6. What items are prohibited for donation? +
Any expired, spoiled, or potentially unsafe food, as well as food containing allergens without proper labeling, is not accepted.
7. Who handles the distribution of emergency donations? +
Our team, in collaboration with local NGOs, will ensure that your donation reaches those in need as quickly as possible.
8. Can I donate more than once? +
Absolutely! We welcome recurring donations to assist in ongoing emergency situations. You can set up a recurring donation through our platform.
9. How do I know if my donation was successfully received? +
You will receive a confirmation email or text once your donation has been successfully received and processed.
10. Can I donate other emergency supplies? +
Yes, donations of water, hygiene products, and other supplies are also greatly appreciated. Contact us for specific needs.
